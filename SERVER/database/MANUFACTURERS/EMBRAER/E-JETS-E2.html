Model name: EMBRAER E-JETS E2

Development and production

The Embraer E-Jet E2 family are medium-range jet airliners developed by Embraer, succeeding the
original E-Jet. The program was launched at the Paris Air Show in 2013. The first variant, the E190-E2,
took its first flight on 23 May 2016 and was certified on February 28, 2018 before entering service with
Widerøe on 24 April.

The three twinjet variants share the same four-abreast narrow-body fuselage with different lengths
and three different new wings, Pratt & Whitney PW1000G turbofans in two sizes, fly-by-wire controls
with new avionics, and an updated cabin.
