Model name: AIRBUS A320 Family

Development and production

The Airbus A320 family consists of short- to medium-range, narrow-body, commercial passenger
twin-engine jet airliners by Airbus. The family includes the A318, A319, A320 and A321, as well as
the ACJ business jet. The A320s are also named A320ceo (current engine option) following the
introduction of the A320neo (new engine option). The aircraft family can accommodate up to 236
passengers and has a range of 3,100 to 12,000 km (1,700 to 6,500 nmi), depending on model.

The first member of the A320 family—the A320—was launched in March 1984, first flew on 22 February
1987, and was first delivered in March 1988 to launch customer Air France. The family was extended
to include the A321 (first delivered 1994), the A319 (1996), and the A318 (2003). The A320 family
pioneered the use of digital fly-by-wire flight control systems, as well as side-stick controls, in
commercial aircraft. There has been a continuous improvement process since introduction. Final
assembly of the family takes place in Toulouse, France, and Hamburg, Germany. A plant in Tianjin,
China, has also been producing aircraft for Chinese airlines since 2009, while a final assembly facility
in Mobile, Alabama, United States, delivered its first A321 in April 2016.
